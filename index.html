Traveling to DC

  1. What’s the best way to get to DC?

We recommend taking Amtrak to Union Station in DC if you’re visiting from anywhere in the northeast.  If you’re flying, we recommend that you fly into National Airport/DCA (where you can ride the metro into the city).  If you fly into Dulles Airport, there are buses that can bring you into the city.

  1. Where should we stay if we’re visiting from out of town?

We recommend the following hotels, which are all located near our wedding venue.

  • Washington Hilton - Dupont Circle  
  • Eaton DC - Downtown

  1. What should we do while we’re visiting DC?

There’s so much to see and do in DC, but these are a few of our favorite things.

  • Museums and Historical Sites

You can’t go wrong visiting any of the Smithsonian museums (which are all free for visitors).  We especially recommend the Hirshhorn (for contemporary art), the National Gallery of Art, the National Museum of American History, and the National Museum of African American History and Culture.

The monuments along the National Mall are especially nice to visit at night.  You can rent paddleboats in the Tidal Basin to see some monuments from the water.  Our favorites are the Franklin Delano Roosevelt Memorial and the Martin Luther King, Jr. Memorial, which are next to one another.

  • Outdoor Activities

We love the U.S. National Arboretum and the C&O Canal towpath (including the Billy Goat trail and Maryland side of Great Falls).  There is also a canal boat tour in Georgetown we recommend.  You can see some nice public art along the Metropolitan Branch Trail.

  • Food & Drinks

A few of our favorite places for dinner near Dupont Circle are Agora (Mediterranean), City Lights (Chinese), Archipelago (Chinese food/tiki bar), Osteria Al Volo (Italian), Beau Thai, Spacycloud (Eastern European/vegan), and Jinya (ramen).  If you’ve never had Ethiopian food, we would encourage you to try Chercher, Letena, or Habesha Market.  

Call Your Mother (bagels), Tryst (coffeehouse/cafe), Little Sesame (Mediterranean), Busboys & Poets (eclectic), and Tatte (bakery/cafe) are all favorites for breakfast, lunch, and snacks.  NuVegan (vegan soul food) is great at any time of day.  And we love Mount Desert Island Ice Cream and Jeni’s for dessert.

Teaism and Philz Coffee are great for tea and coffee.  Union Market is a fun place to visit and check out a variety of local vendors.
